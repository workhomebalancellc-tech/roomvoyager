## Nouvel Hôtel Paris — Charm in the 12th

Tucked into a quiet street in the 12th arrondissement, [Nouvel Hôtel Paris](https://expedia.com/affiliates/workhomebalance_llc/parisdeal1_1) is one of those finds that makes you feel like a local the moment you check in. Originally built as a boarding school for girls, the building has held onto its old-world Parisian character — think high ceilings, warm interiors, and a calm that's surprisingly rare in the heart of the city.

The 12th is a neighborhood that visitors often overlook, and that's exactly what makes it special. You're far enough from the tourist crowds to breathe, but close enough to everything that matters. The Bel-Air metro stop is a three-minute walk from the front door, putting the Eiffel Tower, the Marais, and the Seine all within easy reach. Place de la Nation — one of Paris's great public squares, surrounded by cafés and brasseries — is just a 20-minute stroll away.

Rooms are cozy in the way that Parisian rooms always are (read: small, but perfectly designed), and the hotel earns its reputation for cleanliness and service. Guests consistently rate it highly for value, and at prices that won't make you wince, it's one of the most affordable ways to stay in central Paris.

## Artemisia Montmartre — Art Deco in the Shadow of Sacré-Cœur

If Nouvel Hôtel is about understated Parisian living, [Artemisia Montmartre](https://expedia.com/affiliates/workhomebalance_llc/parisdeal1_2) is about the full romantic experience. Set just 656 feet from the Moulin Rouge and steps from the winding cobblestone streets of the Butte Montmartre, this boutique hotel sits in one of the most iconic neighborhoods on earth.

Renovated in 2023, the Artemisia leans fully into its Art Deco and Art Nouveau bones — think ornate ironwork, warm lighting, and rooms that feel like they belong in a film. Guests rate it 8.8 out of 10 on Booking.com and 9.2 on Hotels.com, with reviews that consistently call out the comfortable beds, beautiful decor, and staff that goes out of their way to make your stay memorable.

Montmartre itself is worth the trip alone. The neighborhood sits on a hill above Paris, and once you've climbed the steps to Sacré-Cœur and looked out over the city at sunset, you'll understand why artists have been coming here for over a century. The streets below are full of crêperies, wine bars, and tiny galleries that could keep you busy for days. And at night? The Moulin Rouge is literally around the corner.

Being between the Opéra and Montmartre districts also means you're well-positioned for everything from Galeries Lafayette shopping to an evening at the opera — without ever needing to rush.

**Best for:** Couples, design lovers, anyone who wants the classic "movie Paris" experience, and return visitors ready to go deeper into the city's soul.
